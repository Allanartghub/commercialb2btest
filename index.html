FairChain advantage

Your machine. Moyee coffee. A better supply chain.

Most office coffee suppliers stop at equipment. Moyee connects your machine with FairChain coffee, beans roasted at origin in Ethiopia and Kenya, where the roasting jobs, the skills, and a greater share of the profit stay in the country that grew the coffee. We pay farmers at least 20% above the market floor, and our entire supply chain is built around the principle that producers should share in the value of what they grow.

For businesses with sustainability targets, ESG reporting obligations, or a genuine commitment to ethical procurement, Moyee's FairChain model is a practical and credible choice. We are B Corp certified with a score of 121.2, one of the highest in the food and beverage sector in Ireland, and every bag of FairChain coffee carries that accountability behind it.

FAQs

Questions businesses ask before choosing a machine.

Which coffee machine is best for a 10-person office? +
For a team of 5–20 people, the Jura W8 is Moyee's most popular office machine. It brews espresso, americano, and milk drinks automatically, needs no dedicated operator, and suits a shared kitchen with moderate daily volume. It pairs directly with Moyee FairChain coffee beans and includes setup support. If your team drinks more than 40 cups a day, we'd recommend stepping up to the Jura X10.
Which coffee machine is best for a 50-person office? +
A team of 50 people typically needs a higher-capacity bean-to-cup machine capable of handling 80–120 drinks per day. The Jura GIGA X3c is designed exactly for this — it runs two grinders simultaneously, supports espresso, americano, flat white and hot chocolate, and is built for continuous use in busy workplace environments. For teams of 50–100 people it is Moyee's most recommended office machine.
Is maintenance included with a Moyee coffee machine? +
Every Moyee office setup includes installation support, cleaning guidance, and care instructions. Servicing and ongoing maintenance packages are available depending on your machine and usage level — this is confirmed as part of your quote. All Jura machines also come with manufacturer warranty cover, and Moyee can advise on extended servicing options for high-volume workplaces.
Can we lease or buy a Moyee coffee machine? +
Moyee machines are available to purchase outright, with prices starting from €2,456 for full bean-to-cup office machines. Lease and monthly rental options are available for businesses that prefer a lower upfront cost, typically starting from €[X] per month depending on the machine and package. Get in touch for a tailored quote that includes machine, coffee supply and support.
Does the machine work with Moyee FairChain coffee? +
Yes. All Moyee office machines are selected to work with Moyee FairChain coffee beans, and our recommended setups include a matched coffee supply as standard. FairChain coffee is roasted at origin in Ethiopia and Kenya — which means more of the value from your daily coffee spend stays in the country where it was grown. For businesses with ESG or sustainability goals, this is a straightforward way to improve your supply chain impact without changing your morning routine.
Can Moyee supply multiple office locations? +
Yes. Moyee supplies office coffee solutions across multiple locations for businesses with more than one site. This includes machine setup, consistent FairChain coffee delivery, and a single account relationship for all locations. Multi-site requirements are handled through a tailored business quote — select "Multi-site / enterprise" in the form below and we will be in touch within one business day.
What is the difference between bean-to-cup and commercial espresso coffee machines? +
A bean-to-cup machine automates the entire process, grinding, tamping, brewing, and milk frothing, at the touch of a button, with no barista required. It is the right choice for most offices where staff want quality coffee quickly without any training or skill involved. A commercial espresso machine is a professional-grade piece of equipment designed for barista-led environments like cafés, restaurants, and hospitality venues — it produces a superior espresso but requires a trained operator to get consistent results.
